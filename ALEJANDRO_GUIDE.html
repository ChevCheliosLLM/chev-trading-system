02 — Interactive

Follow one setup through the entire pipeline

Click each step to expand it. This is the exact order the code runs in — nothing here is simplified or reordered for narrative convenience.

1
Every ~2 minutes, Dexter scans every pair × timeframe combination
DEXTER
▶

Crypto scans 5m / 15m / 30m / 1h / 4h. Forex and stocks scan 15m / 30m / 1h / 4h only — no 5m, the data's too thin to trust at that speed. Each (symbol, timeframe) pair only re-scans when its own candle has actually closed, not on a flat timer — a 4H pair isn't re-evaluated every 2 minutes, only every 4 hours.

If a symbol already has an open trade, it's skipped from new scans entirely for that symbol — Dexter doesn't pile more scrutiny onto something he's already managing.

2
engines.py / patterns.py / derivs.py do the actual measuring
DEXTER
▶

Dexter never eyeballs a chart — everything is a number produced by a dedicated module:

  • engines.py — swing highs/lows, SR zone touch counts, Fibonacci anchors, Volume Profile bins, RSI divergence pivots.
  • patterns.py — chart pattern detection (head & shoulders, double top/bottom, triangles, flags) with a confidence score.
  • derivs.py — crypto-only: Binance funding rate (read as a contrarian signal — the crowded side pays to stay in, which is squeeze fuel against them) and open-interest trend (price+OI direction agreement/disagreement).
3
Every signal gets turned into points, then multiplied by timeframe
DEXTER
▶

Full breakdown in section 03. The short version: SR, Golden Pocket, Volume Profile, and confirmed RSI divergence are the heavyweight signals (3–4pt); EMA position, Bollinger Band state, chart patterns, and Fibonacci add smaller amounts; a few things (BB squeeze, RSI 50-cross, "forming" divergence) are shown to Chev as context but score zero points on purpose, because scoring them contradicted the playbook rule to avoid entering during them.

4
The gates — most setups die right here, before Chev is ever asked
GATE
▶

Full breakdown in section 04. In order: circuit breaker → rate-limit/Chev-health check → cooldown → forex event block → choppy-regime filter → the minimum-worth-evaluating pre-check → the four-part Tradeability Pre-Gate (score threshold, structural anchor, distance from level, directional conflict). Every rejection here is shadow-logged to the Examiner with a specific reason code — nothing just silently vanishes.

5
If it survives everything above, Chev gets asked
CHEV
▶

Full breakdown in section 05. Chev receives his own playbook, recent journal entries, current open positions, a full market brief (price, Fib, SR, Volume Profile, RSI, BOS/CHoCH, session context), and an explicit set of tools he's instructed to call before deciding. He replies in a strict format — see section 06.

6
If Chev says POST, risk_gauntlet.py decides what actually happens
GATE
▶

Full breakdown in section 07. Chev's requested size, risk %, and leverage are treated as a request, not an instruction — 14 sequential checks (equity floor, side sanity, ATR data quality, stale price, ATR floor, risk-band clamp by setup grade, cost model, net R:R after costs, sizing, leverage derived from margin budget, liquidation safety, portfolio heat cap, correlation cap, concurrency cap) can adjust or outright reject the trade before it's logged.

7
Logged to Google Sheets, then managed candle-by-candle
DEXTER
▶

Full breakdown in section 08. Exits are checked against real candle wicks and gaps (honest_sim.py), not just a spot price poll — a stop can be hit and recover between two-minute checks, and this system catches that instead of missing it. Partial take-profit, stop-in-profit, and the newer level-health trade management all live here.

8
On close: Chev reviews himself, and the Examiner grades everyone
CHEV
▶

Full breakdown in section 09. Every closed trade gets a forced post-mortem from Chev, a mechanical MAE/MFE/RSI-path forensic report, and — separately, silently, for every setup Dexter ever saw (POST, SKIP, or blocked before Chev even heard about it) — a triple-barrier label from the Examiner (labeller.py), which is what eventually tells us whether Chev's judgment is actually adding anything over the raw score.

03 — The Scoring System

How Dexter scores a setup

Score = raw points × a timeframe multiplier. The multiplier makes higher timeframes structurally worth more — the same signal on 4H needs to clear a smaller relative bar than on 15m to be taken just as seriously.

×1.2
4H
×1.0
1H (and 5m, no TF entry)
×0.9
30M
×0.9
15M
Support / Resistance
3pt / 2pt
A zone touched 3+ times scores 3pt (multi-touch); touched 1–2 times scores 2pt. If both a resistance and support zone are in range at once, only the nearer one scores — price sandwiched mid-range isn't double confluence, it's chop.
Golden Pocket
3pt
The 0.618 Fibonacci retracement zone. Stacking with an SR zone at the same price triggers a named "GP×SR deadly combo" with a ×1.15 quality bonus.
Volume Profile (POC/VAH/VAL)
3pt / 1.5pt
Full points if the underlying auction anchor is confirmed with follow-through; half points if the anchor is active but not yet confirmed (marked "unconfirmed-anchor" in the reason string, still visible to Chev).
RSI Divergence (confirmed)
3pt / 4pt
3pt for regular or hidden; 4pt if both fire together. Only the two highest-scoring divergences that share the same directional bias can both score — a bullish and bearish divergence no longer cancel out into "confluence."
EMA (55 / 21 / 13)
2 / 1 / 0.5pt
Support/resistance test against each EMA period, weighted by which EMA (55 > 21 > 13). Total EMA contribution is capped at 3pt so a triple-EMA stack can't dominate the score on its own.
Bollinger Bands
2 / 0.5 / 1pt
BURST (price outside the band) = 2pt; near-band = 0.5pt; mid-band = 1pt. Squeeze scores 0pt — every playbook says don't enter during a squeeze, so it's shown as context only, never used to push a setup over threshold.
Derivatives (crypto only)
0–4.5pt
Funding extreme (contrarian, crowded side = squeeze risk): 2–3pt. OI divergence (price/OI disagree — weak move): 1.5pt. OI confirmation (price/OI agree — real participation): 1pt.
Chart Pattern / Sweep / Fib / RSI level
0.5–3pt
Pattern: 2pt (high-conf) / 1pt (mid-conf). Liquidity sweep: 3pt. Fib retracement (non-golden-pocket): 1.5pt. RSI overbought/oversold: 0.5pt. RSI 50-cross and "forming" divergence are shown to Chev but score 0pt — context, not confluence.
04 — The Filter Before Chev

The gates — this is where almost everything dies

A high score alone doesn't reach Chev. Every one of these runs first, in this exact order, and every rejection is shadow-logged with a specific reason code so the Examiner can measure whether each gate is actually helping.

Circuit breaker
→
If today's accumulated realized R has fallen to −3.0R or worse, no new escalations happen for the rest of the UTC day — a new day auto-clears it. This blocks new risk only; open trades keep being managed exactly as normal. One Telegram alert is sent per halted day, not one per scan cycle. Newest gate — wired in tonight, not live until Dexter restarts.
Rate limit / Chev health
→
If Open WebUI 429'd Dexter recently, or is unreachable, escalation is paused until the cooldown clears or a health check succeeds.
Cooldown
→
Per symbol+timeframe. 10 min after a SKIP or malformed reply; much longer (up to 24h on 4H) after a POST — the pair is live, no need to re-hammer it.
Forex event block
→
No new trades within 30 minutes of a high-impact release (FOMC/CPI/NFP/GDP/PCE keywords), extended to crypto/stocks too.
Choppy regime
→
4H ADX below 15 means no directional edge — random noise, skipped outright.
Worth-evaluating pre-check (fixed tonight)
→
A cheap first-pass filter, per timeframe — is the score even in the neighbourhood of being interesting? Until tonight this used a hardcoded table (5/5/6/6/7) that never read Exploration Mode or asset type at all — a real inconsistency, since every other gate is required to route through the same helper. Fixed to scale off the actual live threshold instead.
Tradeability Pre-Gate (4 parts)
→
1. Score below threshold — must clear 7 (crypto) / 6 (forex, stock) right now, exploration numbers.
2. No structural anchor — needs SR score ≥2 or VP score ≥2. Kills mid-range EMA/Fib-only setups floating with nothing solid underneath.
3. Too far from the level — must be within 1.5% of the confluence zone, unless it's a VP anchor (the VP zone is its own location).
4. Conflicting signals — if the minor directional side scores ≥2pt and the dominant side isn't at least 2× it, that's contradiction, not confluence.
05 — The Escalation Message

What Dexter actually puts in front of Chev

One long structured message, assembled fresh every time. Chev is told the setup already passed the gates — his job from here is judgment, not re-counting points.

ask_chev_to_judge() message contents, in order
1. Playbook
→
Chev's own asset-specific playbook (crypto / forex / stocks) — a living document rewritten by the learning loop every 10 closed trades.
2. Recent journal + open positions
→
Last few relevant journal entries, plus every currently open/pending trade with entry/SL/TP/R:R and a correlation note.
3. Re-entry pattern warning
→
If the same level + same tags failed recently (2h window, last 30 losses), a PATTERN ALERT fires so Chev doesn't repeat a fresh mistake.
4. Full market brief
→
4H macro trend verdict, price/VWAP/EMA/RSI/ATR/volume/BB snapshot, Fib levels, multi-TF SR zones with touch counts, Volume Profile, prior day H/L/C, ADR usage, RSI divergence (confirmed + forming), BOS/CHoCH events, zone dwell history, MACD.
5. Counter-trend alert (if applicable)
→
Fires when the setup fights the 4H regime. 5-point checklist including a score requirement of 1.5× the normal per-asset threshold (never the exploration one) and a confirmed — not forming — divergence.
6. Tools available
→
Explicit instructions to call at least one live tool (get_support_resistance, get_volume_profile, detect_rsi_divergence, etc.) before deciding — never narrated, always actually called.
7. Trading rules + absolute rules
→
SL placement, R:R expectations, entry modes (confirmation vs. pending-limit), and the hard non-negotiables: SL must be on the structurally correct side, R:R ≥ 2.0, structural SL only.
06 — The Reply Contract

How Chev must respond

Strict format. If the reply doesn't start with POST: or SKIP:, Dexter retries once with a reminder before giving up on that escalation.

POST: direction=long, entry=X, sl=Y, tp=Z, risk_pct=N, leverage=N,
    position_size_usd=N, tags=sr_4h,fib_1h,rsi, trade_type=day,
    sr_level=X, fib_high=X, fib_low=X, vp_start=YYYY-MM-DD

REASONING: <full analysis>
  Must include 4H STRUCTURE / INVALIDATION / CONFIRMATION blocks
SKIP: <must name ONE concrete broken element>
  e.g. "no invalidation level", "counter-trend without meeting the MTF tax conditions"
  NEVER valid: general caution, "not enough confluence", a letter grade alone

Numbers in the TRADE: line (risk_pct, leverage, position_size_usd) are a request. The real, final numbers are computed by risk_gauntlet.py in the next step — see section 07. tags= controls which anchors get drawn on the chart (sr_level, fib_high/low, vp_start) and which features the Examiner's tag-lift report tracks — it does not affect the score, which was already decided before Chev was asked.

07 — After Chev Says Yes

risk_gauntlet.py — 14 checks, in order

This module has grown well beyond a simple sanity check. Leverage in particular is never Chev's choice — it's a number derived from margin budget and safety caps, regardless of what he requested.

1. Equity floor
→
No new trades below $100 total equity (free cash + reserved margin).
2. Side sanity
→
SL/TP must be on the structurally correct side of entry for the stated direction — malformed trades are rejected outright.
3. ATR data quality
→
ATR must be 0.005%–10% of price — outside that band, the candle data itself is untrustworthy and the trade is refused, not patched around.
4. Stale price guard
→
If price has already consumed >50% of the entry→SL distance while Chev was deliberating, the R:R he judged no longer exists.
5. ATR floor
→
SL distance must be ≥ 1.0× ATR (scalp) / 1.2× (day) / 1.5× (swing) — otherwise it's fixable: widen SL or reclassify trade_type.
6. Risk clamp by grade
→
Chev's requested risk % is clamped into a band by setup grade: B = 0.5–1.0%, A = 1.0–1.75%, A+ = 1.75–2.5%.
7. Cost model
→
Round-trip cost expressed in R-multiples must stay under 0.20R (scalp) / 0.25R (day) / 0.30R (swing) — a stop too tight lets fees eat the trade alive.
8. Net R:R
→
R:R after costs must clear 2.0 (scalp) / 1.8 (day) / 2.5 (swing) — fixable by revising TP/SL.
9–10. Size & leverage
→
Notional is computed from risk $ ÷ stop distance. Leverage is then derived from the margin budget (10% of equity max per trade) and capped by asset/trade type (crypto 10x scalp/day, 5x swing; forex/stock 5x scalp/day, 2x swing) — never simply "whatever Chev asked for."
11. Liquidation safety
→
Stop distance must stay under 50% ÷ leverage; leverage is stepped down automatically to satisfy this before rejecting outright.
12–14. Portfolio caps
→
Total open risk capped at 8% of equity; max 2 same-direction correlated crypto positions; concurrency capped per trade type (1 scalp, 2 day, 2 swing) at once.
08 — While the Trade is Live

Managing an open trade

Exits are checked against real candle wicks and gaps, not a 2-minute spot-price poll — a stop can be pierced and recover between polls, and a naive spot check would miss that entirely.

🕯️
honest_sim.py — candle-true exits
Every management cycle

Applies a strict rulebook per candle: gap-through-SL exits at the worse open price; an ambiguous candle touching both SL and TP always resolves SL first (pessimism); gap-through-TP exits at TP, never better than quoted. Real trading costs (fees + slippage, funding for crypto swings) are deducted on every close via the same cost model risk_gauntlet uses.

🩺
Level-Health Management
New this session — replaces the old R-distance milestone trigger

The old rule fired a partial-close or a Chev-ask purely on how far price had travelled (1R) — which tells you nothing about whether the trade is about to stall or break through. The new rule instead grades the actual level in front of the trade (TP itself, or an SR zone between entry and TP) as HOLDING, WEAKENING, or BROKEN — reusing the same confirmed/active engine originally built to validate entries.

🛡️
SIP — Stop In Profit
Once the SL crosses the entry price

Once the SL has been trailed into profit territory, the trade mathematically cannot lose money anymore. Milestone alerts fire at 50%/75% progress toward TP or the (SIP) floor, prompting Chev to review whether to trail further.


BROKEN
Level failed to hold
SL → breakeven, let it run
WEAKENING
Losing structure, not broken yet
Ask Chev, with the verdict in his prompt
HOLDING
Strong, confirmed, likely to reject price
Close 50% mechanically, trail SL to entry
09 — The Learning Loop

When a trade closes

A close isn't the end of the data — it's the start of grading it. Three separate systems fire, none of which affect the trade itself anymore.

📝
Chev's post-mortem
Every closed trade

Chev is asked to grade his own call in a fixed structure: setup quality, what price actually did, one specific observation, and (if he made in-trade management calls) a review of those decisions. Ends with a VALIDATED / PARTIAL / INVALIDATED verdict.

🔬
trade_forensics.py
Mechanical, no opinion

Reconstructs MAE (max adverse excursion) and MFE (max favourable excursion) in R, whether an SL hit was a "wick" or a "close," whether price later reached the original TP after stopping out, and RSI at entry/exit. Feeds a statistics block into the learning session once ≥10 samples exist.

⚖️
The Examiner (labeller.py)
Every setup, not just Chev's trades

A shadow triple-barrier label gets built for every setup Dexter ever sees — POST, SKIP, or blocked before Chev even heard about it — so it's possible to measure whether Chev's judgment adds anything above the raw Dexter score, not just how the trades he took performed.

Every 10 closed trades per asset type, a learning session rewrites that asset's playbook — extracting loss patterns, win/loss insight comparisons, and (once ≥10 forensics samples exist) a statistical block on wick-vs-close stop-outs and stopped-then-ran frequency. Every claim in that rewrite is required to state its sample size — no absolute language below n=20.

Two real, separate things were found and fixed tonight while investigating this:

Found & fixed — duplicate process

Two independent dexter.py processes were running at once, both launched at the same second (20:28:50) — not Flask's reloader (that's explicitly disabled), a genuine accidental double-launch. Two bots writing to the same Google Sheet and hitting Chev's API simultaneously risks rate-limit contention and cooldown-state desync between the two, since neither knows the other exists. Both were stopped — restart a single instance manually.

Found & fixed — inconsistent threshold

The first-pass "worth evaluating" filter used a flat, hardcoded per-timeframe table that never read Exploration Mode or asset type — a real violation of this codebase's own rule that every score check must route through one shared helper. Fixed to scale off the real live threshold. Important nuance: for crypto specifically, the old hardcoded numbers happened to already sit at or below the exploration threshold, so this fix is a correctness/consistency improvement, not a smoking gun that explains tonight's silence on its own.

The more likely honest explanation for the last four hours specifically: it's Friday evening moving into Saturday UTC — forex and stock markets are already closed for the weekend (so only crypto is scanning at all right now), and crypto liquidity typically thins out into the weekend, which shows up exactly as "plenty of scans, none of them structurally convincing enough to clear the pre-gate." That's the gates working as designed on a quiet market, not a bug suppressing good setups. Worth watching over the next day: does escalation volume pick back up once weekday liquidity returns, now that the duplicate process is gone and the threshold fix is in? 12 — Quick Lookup

Glossary

Every term and reason code that shows up in the logs, in one place.

EXPLORATION_MODE
One boolean in dexter.py. Lowers entry thresholds so the learning loop gets enough data; every downstream safety gate (sizing, SL sanity, circuit breaker) is untouched by it.
NOT_ESCALATED
Shadow-logged decision meaning Chev was never asked — a gate stopped it first. Always has a reason code attached.
below_threshold / below_trade_threshold
The old flat per-TF pre-check vs. the newer, real per-asset trade threshold. Now both route through the same helper.
struct_pregate
Rejected for having no structural anchor — SR score and VP score both below 2.
too_far_from_level / conflicting_signals
Price isn't close enough to the confluence zone yet, or the bullish/bearish points genuinely contradict each other.
STRUCT_REJECT
A second, legacy structural check that still fires after Chev replies — redundant with struct_pregate on purpose, both measured separately.
SIP (Stop In Profit)
SL has been trailed past entry into profit territory — the trade can no longer lose money.
HOLDING / WEAKENING / BROKEN
Level-health verdict on the zone ahead of a running trade — confirmed & strong, losing structure, or already failed.
R-multiple
PnL expressed as a multiple of the dollar risk taken on the trade. +2R = twice the amount risked, gained.
MAE / MFE
Max Adverse / Favourable Excursion — how far a trade moved against or in favour of the position in R, at any point before exit.
Circuit breaker
Halts new escalations for the rest of the UTC day once realized R hits −3.0. Never affects open trades.
The Examiner
labeller.py — builds a shadow triple-barrier label for every setup Dexter ever sees, POST/SKIP/blocked alike, to measure whether Chev's judgment adds real value.
Jane
A second, parallel paper-trading account tracking Kev's own manual trades (via Telegram), which Chev also gives an advisory verdict on for comparison.
