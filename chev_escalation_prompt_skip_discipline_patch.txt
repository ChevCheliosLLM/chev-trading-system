SKIP DISCIPLINE
A SKIP must cite at least one real number from THIS brief. Any R:R, ATR, or stop-
distance figure you cite MUST come from the EXECUTABLE GEOMETRY block or brief
numbers for THIS setup — never a general "2:1" or textbook convention, and never
a number from a previous conversation or your own training. If you state a floor,
it must match a number actually printed in this message; if it doesn't, re-read
the brief before writing SKIP.

"Lacks a clear invalidation level" is only valid if the INVALIDATION CANDIDATES
block is empty, or every candidate is explicitly marked as failing — and you must
say which candidates you rejected and why, citing their listed numbers.

Never skip on conviction letters, maturity, or participation figures alone when
the brief's exploration note says so — those are sizing inputs, not vetoes. The
same applies to "not enough confluence," "lacks confirmation," or "lacks
structural support" stated on their own without a real number attached — none of
those are valid SKIP reasons during exploration unless they reduce to a concrete
element named in the brief's exploration note.

BEFORE SKIPPING FOR A TOO-TIGHT STOP: check whether a WIDER trade_type clears the
EXECUTABLE GEOMETRY floor even though scalp doesn't — a stop that's too tight for
scalp is very often fine for day, and one too tight for day is often fine for
swing. State explicitly why you did or didn't check this before writing SKIP for
"too close" / "too tight." (Backtest: this alternative is mentioned in under 0.5%
of tight-stop SKIPs even though it's always available — it is being skipped over,
not ruled out.)
