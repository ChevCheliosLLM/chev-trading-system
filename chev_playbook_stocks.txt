### CONFLUENCE CONDITIONS
- Stocks require daily/weekly SR levels as the primary anchor — intraday levels alone are too noisy. A weekly resistance level tested 3+ times is a wall; an intraday 15m level is context only.
- Chart patterns (head and shoulders, wedge, ascending channel) are more reliable in stocks than in forex or crypto because institutional algos respond to classic patterns. Breakout + volume confirmation is the highest conviction signal.
- Earnings and macro news override all technical setups. Never hold through earnings unless the setup is specifically an earnings play with defined risk. Check the economic calendar before entering.

### TRENDING MARKET BEHAVIOUR — CRITICAL
- In a confirmed uptrend (4H HH/HL, net move > +3%), resistance levels are CONTINUATION ZONES, not reversal zones. Stocks in strong uptrends break resistance with force — shorting resistance in an uptrend is almost always wrong.
- In a confirmed downtrend (4H LH/LL, net move < -3%), support levels break down. Longing support in a downtrend is similarly low probability.
- Counter-trend trades require ALL of: (1) A+ score ≥ 1.5× the normal threshold (stocks = 12), (2) confirmed RSI divergence on 1H or 4H, (3) explicit 4H BOS or CHoCH proving trend reversal. Any one missing → SKIP.
- Dexter will show a '4H Macro Trend Context' block at the top of every market brief. Read it before assessing individual S/R levels.

### MARKET STRUCTURE RULES
- Stocks gap at open. A gap fill setup (price opens above/below previous close and reverts) is a reliable intraday pattern — the gap is a vacuum with no traded price, so price often fills it.
- Pre-market and after-hours moves define the opening range. The first 15-30 minutes after open establish direction. Wait for the opening range to define before entering a trend trade.
- Volume at key levels is the differentiator in stocks. A breakout above resistance on double average volume is real. The same breakout on low volume is likely a false break.

### ENTRY QUALITY STANDARDS
- Stocks SL minimum: 1.0× ATR scalp | 1.5× ATR day | 2.5× ATR swing. Stocks gap more than forex but less than crypto — use daily ATR for day trades, not intraday ATR.
- The best stock entries align intraday structure (15m / 1H) with daily chart direction. Against the daily trend, even technically perfect intraday setups underperform.
- Stocks have lower leverage than forex/crypto. Typical leverage: 2–4× for intraday, 1–2× for swing. Scale position size up vs forex to compensate for lower leverage and smaller % moves.

### ASSET NOTES
- US stock market hours: 09:30–16:00 EST. Pre-market 04:00–09:30, after-hours 16:00–20:00. Liquidity is highest in the first and last hour of the main session.
- AMZN, NVDA, and mega-caps tend to respect daily/weekly levels cleanly. Small caps are more volatile with thinner orderbooks — levels break more easily.
- Dividends and stock splits affect historical price levels on charts. If a level looks unusual, check for a corporate action before trusting it.
