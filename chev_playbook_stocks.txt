### TRENDING MARKET BEHAVIOUR — CRITICAL
- In a confirmed uptrend (4H HH/HL, net move > +3%), resistance levels are CONTINUATION ZONES, not reversal zones. Stocks in strong uptrends break resistance with force — shorting resistance in an uptrend is almost always wrong.
- In a confirmed downtrend (4H LH/LL, net move < -3%), support levels break down. Longing support in a downtrend is similarly low probability.
- Counter-trend trades require ALL of: (1) A+ score ≥ 1.5× the normal threshold (stocks = 12), (2) confirmed RSI divergence on 1H or 4H, (3) explicit 4H BOS or CHoCH proving trend reversal. Any one missing → SKIP.
- Dexter will show a '4H Macro Trend Context' block at the top of every market brief. Read it before assessing individual S/R levels.

- High-confidence patterns like EMA and fast anchor combinations are more likely to reach TP in trending markets.
- Patterns that break out from strong support or resistance levels have a higher chance of success.

- Marginal setups lack strong confluence or have conflicting signals from different time frames.
