### STRUCTURAL RISK FACTORS
- **Ranging Market Conditions**: High volatility and lack of directional bias in ranging markets can lead to choppy price action, making setups with tight stop-loss placements more prone to being hit. Require a broader structural support/resistance band or an extended timeframe confirmation before entering.
  
- **Bollinger Bands Squeeze (BBS)**: Presence of BBS indicates high volatility risk, which may cause rapid price movements that can quickly trigger stop losses. Look for additional confluences like strong SR levels or clear momentum indicators to mitigate this risk.

- **Marginal Entry Levels**: Entries near the edges of structural support/resistance zones without a clear confirmation from other indicators (e.g., RSI divergence, EMA crossovers) are more susceptible to being invalidated by minor price fluctuations. Require at least two additional confluences or a broader SR level for higher confidence.
